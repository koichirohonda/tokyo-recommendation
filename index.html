Explore by Area

Each Tokyo neighborhood has its own personality. Here's what to do in each.

Harajuku / Shibuya / Omotesando

🛍️ Shopping

  • Harajuku: Young/vintage fashion in small alley shops, Cat Street
  • Omotesando: High-end brand boulevard, Omotesando Hills
  • Shibuya: Miyashita Park, Shibuya Hikarie

🏛️ Attractions

  • Meiji Shrine: Serene traditional Shinto shrine in a forest
  • Shibuya Scramble Crossing: One of the world's busiest intersections
  • Shibuya Sky: Open-air observation deck, famous on social media

🍜 Food & Nightlife

  • Harajuku Gyoza (Dumplings)
  • Daihachi Takohachimaru (Takoyaki)
  • TOKYO Whisky Library (Bar, Omotesando)
  • Trunk Hotel (Lounge Cafe & Bar)

Roppongi & Nishiazabu

🎨 Art

  • Mori Art Museum & Tokyo City View
  • The National Art Center
  • Suntory Museum of Art

🍽️ Food

  • Ushigoro: Premium Yakiniku BBQ (~$80)
  • Sushi Tsu: $50 lunch, $200 dinner
  • Multiple restaurants listed in the Restaurants tab

🍸 Nightlife

  • Gen Yamamoto: Cocktail set menu (4 or 6 drinks) — book ahead
  • One Oak: Popular nightclub

Ginza

🛍️ Shopping

  • Ginza Six: Tsutaya bookstore (6F), food hall (B2F)
  • Mitsukoshi: Underground food paradise
  • MUJI Flagship: Shop + restaurant + hotel
  • Flagship stores: RIMOWA, ISSEY MIYAKE, BAO BAO

🍽️ Food

  • World-class Michelin-starred sushi restaurants
  • Sato Yosuke: Inaniwa-style udon
  • Glitch Coffee & Sadaharu Aoki

Asakusa & Kappabashi

🏛️ Traditional Tokyo

  • Senso-ji Temple: Popular Buddhist temple built in the 7th century
  • Kaminarimon: Iconic Thunder Gate
  • Nakamise Shopping Street: Traditional souvenirs & street food
  • Asakusa Hanayashiki: One of Japan's oldest amusement parks

🔪 Kappabashi (Kitchen Street)

  • Kamaasa: THE knife shop (English-speaking staff)
  • Numata Nori: Seaweed specialist
  • Food Sample shops: Make your own plastic food replicas
  • ⚠️ Best on weekdays. Avoid Sunday (70% closed).

Tokyo Station & Marunouchi

🚶 Things to Do

  • Walk around the Imperial Palace Garden
  • Photography at Tokyo Station and Tokyo Station Hotel
  • Shopping at Marunouchi Building and Shin Marunouchi Building

Ebisu / Nakameguro / Daikanyama

🛍️ & 🍽️ Highlights

  • Kinto: Beautiful Japanese ceramics and kitchen goods
  • Tsutaya Daikanyama: Iconic bookstore complex
  • MAISON KITSUNE: Japanese-French fashion
  • Ebisu Yoroniku: Premium yakiniku (book weeks ahead)
  • Udatsu: Michelin-star sushi chef

Tsukiji

🐟 The Fish Market

  • Visit Tsukiji Fish Market early in the morning
  • Sushi Dai: Popular and affordable sushi
  • Various walk-in sushi restaurants
  • Fresh seafood street food vendors

Kuramae

🎨 Creative Neighborhood

  • Doguya Nobori: Beautiful kitchen plates and drinkware
  • Coffee & Bar gg GENIE: Coffee in bear-shaped bottles
  • TOKYO RIVERSIDE DISTILLERY: Craft Japanese gin
